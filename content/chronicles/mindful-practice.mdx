As part of writing the [blueprint](/mark2) page, when I thought of inspiration, the first thing that sprung to mind was [thich nhat hanh](https://plumvillage.org/about/thich-nhat-hanh/biography). More than any author, <i>thich nhat hahn</i> has changed how I process the world around me. Within me.

The way he approaches the ideas of meditation, mindfulness, and mindful practice are easy to latch onto and incorporate, almost immediately. A few quick examples......

How can I process my anger? Through mindful practice. See the feeling, understand the feeling, love the feeling, let the feeling flitter away. Watch the anger go with it. Over and over. Thousands of times. Infinity times.

How can I live a healthier life? Through mindful practice. Instead of eating whatever mindless fast food is easiest, consider the origin. Consider the preparation. Help your mind see that this apple is from a tree, which is from a seed, that took root on earth, as it speeds through the galaxy, expanding out across the universe, this apple is everything. I am this apple. I am everything. I am nothing. This apple is not an apple.

All of this to say, about two weeks ago I went digging through the books I have packed away to make room for cards and other crap, on a mission to find any <i>thich nhat hahn</i> book to read. As I drifted away from my happiest and healthiest self, reading took a back seat, and whatever book I found first of his was going to help me get back on the reading rainbow train.

His books have a few built in advantages on my be less dumb mission:

- Short
- Easy to read
- Mindful practice

Fast forward to last week - I'd read the book here and there, and while I thought it was a stroke of luck to find a book that would help me to connect with eeeeeeeemma's religious experience, I was just chipping away at it. It wasn't really mindful practice.

Then [Thursday hit](/shaolin/this-feeling).

Now I had a mission. I needed to get this book into eeeeeeeemma's hands to help her on her journey. Whether she liked it or not (typically, my book recommendations or gifts are met with an eye roll, hi dad, lol). Anywho, I ripped through it in one shot as soon as I was able.

Okay, that's a lie, I made [this dumb thing](/shaolin/red-eyes) right smack in the middle of the day I finished reading this book. Brought to you by buddha I suppose.

Now I really wanted to share with her how <i>thich nhat hahn</i> tied the idea of meditation, and mindful practice, to the idea of prayer, as well as buddhist breathing exercises to connecting with the holy spirit, let alone his connection of buddha to jesus himself - I knew it could be of great benefit to her. If nothing else, I could hand her a book holding the thing that helped me the most, framed in a way that connects to the world that she is just starting to understand.

My favorite realization out of <i>living buddah, living christ</i> is that it is not about separating yourself as a buddhist, or as a catholic, or as a bucks fan. Instead, the beauty of it all rests in recognizing that my search for peace, is the same search for peace that eeeeeeeemma is drawn to. Two paths to one shared consciousness.

During her initial ER visit, eeeeeeeemma was asked what were some things that lead her to the idea of self-harm and she said "what's the point?". <i>thich nhat hahn</i> taught me to dig deeper when I find myself asking that very same question.

What's the point IS the point.
