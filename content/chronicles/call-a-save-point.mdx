![mewgenics](/images/optimized/call-a-save-point/mewgenics.webp)

Edmund McMillen is an auteur among game developers. So much of every game he's
made has his heart, soul, and vision poured into. Mewgenics is no exception.

I have barely scratched the surface of this game, the level of depth in its
gameplay has barely revealed itself to me. As an avid player of The Binding of
Isaac I am no stranger to learning a game with great depth, but Mewgenics is a
whole other beast entirely. From the different classes your cats can take on
the alley with, and their respective treasure trove of abilities and perks, to
the breeding mechanics a player like myself can't help but figure out ways to
maximize my cats stats. Each aspect of the game shows a level of care and love
from all its contributors that so many games fall short of these days.

And of course, the game is weird as hell. My mom texted me that dad's cats had
attempted to inbreed, only for one to show no interest. All I could assure her
was that there would be plenty of inbreeding to come. The artstyle is gritty
and unapologetic in its depiction of the world and people. The writing is
funny but sad if you think about it too hard. There's a plethora of references
to internet and pop culture that only the keenest of eyes will catch all of.
And for some reason, Rivers Cuomo is here as the voice of some of the cats.

Stellar game.
